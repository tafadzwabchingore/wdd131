Zimbabwe is a landlocked country in southern Africa known for its dramatic landscape and diverse wildlife, much of it within parks, reserves and safari areas. The name Zimbabwe is derived from the Shona words, dzimba dzemabwe, meaning houses of stone or stone buildings, symbolizing the Great Zimbabwe stone Ruins near the present day town of Masvingo.
We have 16 official languages and the country is home to the largest man-made lake in the world called Lake Kariba.
